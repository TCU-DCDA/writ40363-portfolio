Welcome to My Portfolio

Introduction

Welcome to my digital portfolio. I am a professor in the English department at Texas Christian University and a creative practitioner exploring the intersections between traditional literary forms and emerging digital media.

My work spans poetry published in prestigious journals like The Sun Magazine and Florida Review, experimental digital literature projects, and three decades of musical collaboration including current work with The Deep Sleepers.

As an educator, I focus on helping students develop both technical skills and creative thinking through courses like HTML & CSS coding, digital storytelling, and multimedia poetry creation.

This portfolio is part of my work teaching WRIT40363, where I am the instructor in the process of learning how to teach web application development to my students, building accessible, semantic web pages using professional development practices.

Featured Work

My creative practice includes published poetry, digital literature experiments, and musical collaborations. Notable works include "The Renewable Sonnets of William Shakespeare" (New River: Digital Art and Literature, 2021) and "What Falls Away Is Always," a 3D text sculpture rendering Theodore Roethke's poetry as navigable digital space.

In addition to traditional publications in The Sun Magazine and Florida Review, I create interactive digital poetry projects that combine Python, JavaScript, and multimedia elements to explore new forms of literary expression.

Teaching Philosophy

Students consistently praise my supportive teaching approach, clear grading criteria, and ability to make technical subjects accessible. Over the years, students have described my classes as engaging, manageable, and transformative—helping them discover capabilities they never knew they had.

My teaching philosophy centers on providing creative freedom while offering the structured support students need to succeed. Whether it's HTML & CSS coding, digital storytelling, or multimedia poetry creation, I strive to create an environment where students feel empowered to explore and experiment.

For detailed student feedback and testimonials, visit the reviews section on my about page.

Current Learning

With over 30 years of creative practice across poetry, digital literature, and music, I continue exploring new intersections between traditional art forms and emerging technologies. My current projects include collaborative musical work with The Deep Sleepers and experimental digital poetry using TinkerCAD, SketchFab, and interactive web technologies.

Students often note that I am knowledgeable, patient, and supportive, bringing both technical expertise and creative vision to the classroom. My background spans traditional literary publication, digital experimentation, and multimedia collaboration.

As I develop new approaches to teaching web application development, I'm particularly interested in how AI tools can enhance rather than replace human creativity and critical thinking in the digital humanities classroom.
